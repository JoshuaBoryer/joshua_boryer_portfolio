Exploring Data, ANOVA tests, Interpreting model summaries

In this project, I analyzed two datasets using R to understand relationships between variables and make predictions. Chemical Reaction Dataset: I explored how the mass of a chemical changes over time. Using graphs and linear regression, I found a clear positive relationship as the reaction time increases, the mass also increases. I checked the model assumptions and predicted the mass at different time points. Forest Dataset: I compared light levels in Broadleaf and Conifer forests. The analysis showed that Broadleaf forests receive more light, and light decreases as distance from the canopy increases. I created a simple linear model to summarize these findings and make predictions.

Linear Regression, model simplification

In this project, I explored a dataset to understand what affects the risk of infection. Using R, I analyzed relationships between patient stay length, number of X-rays, age, and infection risk. I built a linear regression model and found that longer hospital stays and more X-rays increase the risk of infection, while age had no significant effect. This assignment helped me practice data visualization, model building, and interpreting statistical results.

Hypothesis Testing and Tukey Testing

In this assignment, I analyzed two datasets using R. Air Pollution: I compared air pollution levels across four regions (A, B, C, D) using boxplots and ANOVA tests. Results showed that regions C and D had significantly lower air pollution than A and B, while B and A were not statistically different. Residual analysis confirmed that the linear model assumptions were satisfied. Grass Growth: I examined how sunlight and watering frequency affect grass height. Linear models and ANOVA tests showed that sunlight significantly influences grass height, while watering frequency has little effect. Tukey’s HSD test confirmed which sunlight levels led to taller grass, highlighting moderate sunlight as the most effective. This assignment helped me practice using R for data visualization, hypothesis testing, linear modeling, and interpreting results.
